RETAIL DOMAIN — COMPONENTS, ARCHITECTURE & INTEGRATIONS
=========================================================

## What is the Retail Domain?

  The retail domain covers all systems, processes, and technology
  involved in selling products or services to end consumers —
  through physical stores, e-commerce, mobile apps, or a combination
  (omnichannel retail).

  Modern retail is driven by:
    - Customer experience        : Seamless, personalized shopping journeys.
    - Inventory accuracy         : Right product, right place, right time.
    - Speed                      : Fast order fulfillment and delivery.
    - Data                       : Real-time insights driving decisions.


=========================================================
## CORE RETAIL BUSINESS COMPONENTS

### 1. Product Management
  - Product catalog              : Master list of all products with attributes.
  - SKU management               : Unique identifier per product variant.
  - Pricing                      : Base price, promotions, discounts, dynamic pricing.
  - Product taxonomy             : Categories, sub-categories, attributes.
  - Digital assets               : Images, videos, descriptions per product.

### 2. Inventory Management
  - Stock levels                 : Track quantity across warehouses and stores.
  - Replenishment                : Auto-reorder when stock falls below threshold.
  - Warehouse management         : Location, bin, rack-level tracking.
  - Multi-location inventory     : Stock visibility across all fulfillment nodes.
  - Dead stock / shrinkage       : Identify unsold or lost inventory.

### 3. Order Management
  - Order capture                : Accept orders from all channels.
  - Order routing                : Route to nearest warehouse or store.
  - Order fulfillment            : Pick, pack, ship.
  - Order tracking               : Real-time status updates to customer.
  - Returns & exchanges          : Reverse logistics management.
  - Cancellations                : Handle pre-shipment and post-shipment cancels.

### 4. Customer Management
  - Customer profile             : Identity, contact, preferences, history.
  - Loyalty programs             : Points, rewards, tiers.
  - Segmentation                 : Group customers for targeted campaigns.
  - Customer service             : Support tickets, chat, returns.
  - Customer lifetime value (CLV): Measure long-term revenue per customer.

### 5. Point of Sale (POS)
  - In-store checkout            : Barcode scanning, payment, receipt.
  - Cashier & self-checkout      : Both attended and unattended modes.
  - Returns at POS               : In-store return processing.
  - Store inventory lookup       : Check stock availability at POS.
  - Offline mode                 : Continue sales during network outages.

### 6. E-Commerce / Digital Storefront
  - Product search & browse      : Search, filter, sort, recommendations.
  - Shopping cart                : Add, update, remove items.
  - Checkout flow                : Address, payment, order confirmation.
  - User accounts                : Login, order history, saved addresses.
  - Wishlist & save for later    : Deferred purchase management.

### 7. Pricing & Promotions
  - Base pricing                 : Cost + margin based pricing.
  - Promotional pricing          : % off, buy X get Y, flash sales.
  - Dynamic pricing              : Price changes based on demand/competition.
  - Coupon & voucher management  : Generate, validate, redeem codes.
  - Loyalty discounts            : Member-only pricing.

### 8. Payments
  - Payment gateway              : Process credit/debit, UPI, wallets.
  - Fraud detection              : Flag suspicious transactions.
  - Refunds & chargebacks        : Handle payment reversals.
  - Split payments               : Pay with multiple methods.
  - Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL)     : Deferred payment options.

### 9. Supply Chain & Procurement
  - Vendor management            : Supplier onboarding, contracts, performance.
  - Purchase orders              : Raise, approve, track POs to suppliers.
  - Goods receipt                : Inspect and confirm incoming stock.
  - Supplier portals             : Vendors check PO status and invoices.
  - Demand forecasting           : Predict future stock needs.

### 10. Logistics & Fulfillment
  - Warehouse operations         : Receiving, put-away, picking, packing, shipping.
  - Last-mile delivery           : From warehouse/store to customer.
  - Carrier management           : Rate shopping, carrier assignment, tracking.
  - Returns logistics            : Reverse shipment handling.
  - Same-day / next-day delivery : Express fulfillment workflows.

### 11. Analytics & Reporting
  - Sales reports                : Revenue, units sold, margins.
  - Inventory reports            : Stock levels, turnover rate, aging.
  - Customer analytics           : Behavior, purchase patterns, churn.
  - Campaign performance         : ROI on promotions and marketing.
  - Demand forecasting           : Predict future sales trends.

## RETAIL ARCHITECTURE DESIGN

### Architecture Styles Used in Retail

  1. Monolithic (Legacy Retail)
       All components in one large application.
       Simple but hard to scale. Common in older retail systems.

  2. Microservices (Modern Retail)
       Each domain (orders, inventory, catalog) is a separate service.
       Independent deployment, scaling, and fault isolation.

  3. Headless Commerce
       Frontend (website/app) is decoupled from backend commerce engine.
       Backend exposes APIs; any frontend can consume them.
       Enables faster UI iteration without touching backend.

  4. Composable Commerce
       Best-of-breed services stitched together via APIs.
       Replace any component (e.g., search engine, payment) independently.
       Built on MACH principles: Microservices, API-first, Cloud-native, Headless.

### Core Services in Retail Microservices

  Service               Responsibility
  --------------------  -------------------------------------------------
  Product Catalog       Manage product data, attributes, pricing
  Inventory Service     Track stock levels across all locations
  Order Service         Capture, manage, and fulfill orders

## RETAIL SYSTEM INTEGRATIONS

### 1. POS ↔ Inventory Integration
  - When a sale happens at POS → Inventory Service reduces stock count.
  - When stock falls below threshold → Replenishment order triggered.
  - Integration: Real-time via REST API or Kafka event.

### 2. E-Commerce ↔ Order Management System (OMS)
  - Customer places order on website → OMS captures and processes it.
  - OMS routes order to nearest fulfillment center.
  - Integration: REST API (sync) or Kafka (async).

### 3. OMS ↔ Warehouse Management System (WMS)
  - OMS sends pick list to WMS → WMS executes pick, pack, ship.
  - WMS sends shipment confirmation back to OMS.
  - Integration: Kafka events or REST API.

### 4. WMS ↔ Transport Management System (TMS)
  - WMS triggers shipment → TMS selects carrier, generates label.
  - TMS sends tracking number back to OMS and customer.
  - Integration: REST API + Webhook for tracking updates.

### 5. E-Commerce ↔ Payment Gateway
  - Customer submits payment → Payment Service calls gateway (Stripe, Razorpay).
  - Gateway returns success/failure → Order confirmed or retried.
  - Integration: REST API (sync) + Webhook for async callbacks.

### 6. Product Catalog ↔ Search Engine
  - When product is added/updated → Search index updated in Elasticsearch.
  - Customer searches → Query hits Elasticsearch, not the catalog DB.
  - Integration: Kafka event → Elasticsearch indexer.

### 7. CRM ↔ Marketing Platform
  - Customer data synced from CRM to marketing tools (Salesforce, HubSpot).
  - Campaign results (opens, clicks) synced back to CRM.
  - Integration: Batch sync (nightly ETL) or real-time webhook.

### 8. ERP ↔ Retail Systems
  - Finance data (invoices, payments) flows to ERP (SAP, Oracle).
  - Procurement and vendor data managed in ERP, synced to retail systems.
  - Integration: File-based (EDI/CSV) or REST API.

### 9. Supplier / Vendor Integration
  - Retailers send Purchase Orders to suppliers via EDI or portal.
  - Suppliers confirm delivery, send ASN (Advanced Shipping Notice).
  - Integration: EDI 850/856, SFTP file exchange, or REST API.

### 10. Analytics & Data Warehouse Integration
  - Transactional data (orders, inventory, sales) → ETL pipeline → Data Warehouse.
  - BI tools (Tableau, Power BI) query warehouse for reporting.
  - Integration: Kafka → Spark → Snowflake / Redshift.

### 11. Loyalty Platform Integration
  - Order completed → Points credited to loyalty account.
  - Customer redeems points at checkout → Balance deducted.
  - Integration: REST API (sync at checkout) + async event for accumulation.

### 12. Notification Service Integration
  - Order placed → Send order confirmation email/SMS.
  - Order shipped → Send tracking link.
  - Promotion → Send targeted push notification.
  - Integration: Kafka event → Notification Service → Email/SMS provider (SendGrid, Twilio).

## DATA FLOWS IN RETAIL

### Order-to-Cash Flow
  Customer places order
    → Payment authorized
    → Order confirmed
    → Inventory reserved
    → Warehouse picks & packs
    → Carrier picks up
    → Customer receives delivery
    → Payment settled
    → Invoice generated in ERP

### Procure-to-Pay Flow
  Demand forecast triggers low stock
    → Purchase order raised to supplier
    → Supplier confirms and ships
    → Goods received at warehouse
    → Quality check done
    → Inventory updated
    → Invoice received and paid via ERP

### Return-to-Refund Flow
  Customer initiates return
    → Return authorized (RMA created)
    → Item picked up or dropped at store
    → Item received at warehouse
    → Quality inspected
    → Inventory restocked or disposed
    → Refund processed to customer

## RETAIL INTEGRATION CHALLENGES

  Challenge                   Solution
  --------------------------  ----------------------------------------
  Real-time inventory sync    Kafka events for instant stock updates
  Order routing across nodes  OMS with smart routing rules
  Omnichannel consistency     Centralized catalog and inventory service
  Legacy POS systems          Adapters and middleware (ESB/API layer)
  Supplier data formats       EDI adapters or supplier portals
  Peak load (sales events)    Auto-scaling, message queues, caching
  Data consistency            Saga pattern for distributed transactions
  Fraud prevention            ML-based fraud detection at payment layer

## GOLDEN RULES OF RETAIL ARCHITECTURE

  1. Single source of truth    : One system owns each data domain (inventory, catalog).
  2. Decouple channels         : Web, app, and store share the same backend via APIs.
  3. Design for peak traffic   : Black Friday / Diwali sales need elastic scaling.
  4. Real-time inventory        : Stale stock data leads to overselling and cancellations.
  5. Event-driven first        : Use async messaging to decouple services.
  6. Customer-first design     : Every system decision should improve customer experience.
  7. Automate fulfillment      : Minimize manual steps from order to delivery.
  8. Monitor end-to-end        : Trace every order from placement to delivery.
