HOW TO TALK TO PEOPLE IN THE OFFICE
=====================================

## With Your Manager

  - Give updates proactively    : Don't wait to be asked.
                                  "Here's where I am, here's the blocker."
  - Bring solutions, not problems : "I found an issue. Here are 2 options I'm considering."
  - Ask for clarity early       : "Before I start, let me confirm my understanding..."
  - Use 1:1s well               : Bring a list — priorities, blockers, feedback you need.


## With Peers / Colleagues

  - Be specific when asking for help : "I'm stuck on X, I've tried Y and Z, can you help?"
  - Acknowledge their work      : A simple "that was a good point" builds trust.
  - Disagree respectfully       : "I see it differently — can I share my thinking?"
  - Don't over-message          : Batch non-urgent questions instead of pinging constantly.


## In Meetings

  - Speak early                 : Even one sentence in the first 5 minutes makes it easier to speak again.
  - Be concise                  : State your point, give one reason, stop. Don't over-explain.
  - Ask clarifying questions    : "Can you help me understand what success looks like here?"
  - Avoid filler phrases        : Replace "I just wanted to say..." with just saying it.


## With Senior Leaders

  - Lead with the bottom line   : Conclusion first, details second.
  - Know your numbers           : Be ready to back up what you say.
  - Be confident, not defensive : If you don't know, say "I'll find out and get back to you."


## Everyday Small Talk

  - Start simple                : Comment on something shared (weather, a project, the coffee).
  - Ask open questions          : "How's the project going?" not "Is the project fine?"
  - Remember details            : Follow up on things people mentioned before.
                                  "How did that demo go?"


=====================================
## Golden Rules

  1. Listen more than you speak.
  2. Be clear over being clever.
  3. Match your tone to the context (casual vs. formal).
  4. Follow up in writing after important verbal conversations.
  5. When in doubt, be kind.
