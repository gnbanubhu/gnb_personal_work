MAIL COMMUNICATION GUIDE
=========================

## Email Structure

  1. Subject Line   — clear, specific, actionable
  2. Greeting       — appropriate to the relationship
  3. Opening line   — state the purpose immediately
  4. Body           — context, details, ask
  5. Call to Action — what you need and by when
  6. Closing        — polite sign-off


=========================
## Subject Line Tips

  - Be specific     : "Q2 Report Review — Action Needed by Friday"
                      NOT "Important" or "Quick Question"
  - Use flags       : [ACTION REQUIRED], [FYI], [URGENT], [DECISION NEEDED]
  - Keep it short   : Under 60 characters so it doesn't get cut off.


=========================
## Greetings

  Formal      : "Dear Mr./Ms. [Last Name],"
  Professional: "Hi [First Name],"
  Team/Group  : "Hi Team," / "Hello All,"
  Casual      : "Hey [Name],"


=========================
## Email Types & Templates

### 1. Request Email
  Subject : [ACTION REQUIRED] Request for [Topic] — Due [Date]

  Hi [Name],

  I hope you're doing well.

  I'm writing to request [specific ask].
  [1-2 lines of context/reason].

  Could you please [action] by [date]?

  Let me know if you need any additional information.

  Thanks,
  [Your Name]

---

### 2. Follow-Up Email
  Subject : Follow-Up: [Original Subject]

  Hi [Name],

  I wanted to follow up on my email from [date] regarding [topic].

  [Restate the ask in one line].

  Please let me know if you need anything from my end.

  Thanks,
  [Your Name]

---

### 3. Introduction Email
  Subject : Introduction — [Your Name], [Your Role]

  Hi [Name],

  My name is [Your Name] and I'm [your role/context].

  [1-2 lines about why you're reaching out or how you're connected].

  I'd love to [connect / schedule a call / collaborate on X].

  Looking forward to hearing from you.

  Best regards,
  [Your Name]

---

### 4. Status Update Email
  Subject : Status Update: [Project/Task Name] — [Date]

  Hi [Name/Team],

  Here's a quick update on [project/task]:

  - Completed : [what's done]
  - In Progress: [what's ongoing]
  - Blockers  : [any issues] / None
  - Next Steps: [what's coming]

  Let me know if you have any questions.

  Thanks,
  [Your Name]

---

### 5. Apology / Correction Email
  Subject : Correction / Apology Regarding [Topic]

  Hi [Name],

  I want to apologize for [specific issue].

  [One line acknowledging the impact].

  Here's what I'm doing to fix it: [action].

  It won't happen again. Thank you for your patience.

  Best,
  [Your Name]

---

### 6. Meeting Request Email
  Subject : Meeting Request: [Topic] — [Proposed Date/Time]

  Hi [Name],

  I'd like to schedule a meeting to discuss [topic].

  Proposed time: [Date], [Time], [Duration]
  Agenda:
    1. [Point 1]
    2. [Point 2]

  Please let me know if this works or suggest an alternate time.

  Thanks,
  [Your Name]


=========================
## Tone Guide

  Situation               Tone
  ----------------------  --------------------
  To senior leadership    Formal, concise
  To peers                Professional, friendly
  To direct reports       Clear, supportive
  To external clients     Polite, professional
  To vendors              Direct, specific


=========================
## Common Closings

  Formal      : "Yours sincerely," / "Respectfully,"
  Professional: "Best regards," / "Kind regards,"
  Friendly    : "Thanks," / "Best,"
  Casual      : "Cheers," / "Talk soon,"


=========================
## Golden Rules for Email

  1. One email = one topic. Don't mix multiple requests.
  2. Reply within 24 hours, even if just to acknowledge.
  3. Re-read before sending — check tone, typos, and attachments.
  4. Don't use Reply All unless everyone needs to see it.
  5. Keep it short — if it needs more than 3 paragraphs, pick up the phone.
  6. Never send an angry email — draft it, wait, then decide.
  7. CC sparingly — only include people who truly need to know.
  8. Use BCC when emailing large groups to protect privacy.
