OFFICE MEETINGS GUIDE
======================

## Types of Meetings

  1. Stand-up / Daily Sync     — quick team status update (15 min)
  2. One-on-One (1:1)          — manager & employee check-in
  3. Brainstorming / Ideation  — generating ideas as a group
  4. Decision Meeting          — align and decide on a specific topic
  5. Status Update Meeting     — project/work progress review
  6. Retrospective             — what went well, what to improve
  7. Kick-off Meeting          — start of a new project or initiative
  8. All-Hands / Town Hall     — company-wide announcements and updates


======================
## Before the Meeting

  - Define the purpose         : Know WHY the meeting is happening.
  - Set a clear agenda         : Share it at least 24 hours in advance.
  - Invite the right people    : Only those who are needed.
  - Share pre-reads            : Send documents/data ahead so time isn't wasted reading in the room.
  - Book the right duration    : Default to 30 min; use 60 only if truly needed.


======================
## Meeting Agenda Template

  Meeting Title  : [Topic]
  Date & Time    : [Date], [Time]
  Duration       : [X minutes]
  Attendees      : [Names / Roles]
  Objective      : [What we want to decide or achieve]

  Agenda:
    1. [Topic 1]          — [Time allocated] — [Owner]
    2. [Topic 2]          — [Time allocated] — [Owner]
    3. Action Items Review— 5 min            — All

  Pre-read: [Link or attachment if any]


======================
## During the Meeting

  - Start on time              : Don't wait for late joiners.
  - Appoint a facilitator      : Someone keeps the discussion on track.
  - Appoint a note-taker       : Capture decisions and action items.
  - Stay on agenda             : Park off-topic items in a "parking lot."
  - Encourage participation    : Ask quiet members directly — "What do you think, [Name]?"
  - Avoid side conversations   : Stay focused and present.
  - Summarize decisions        : Before moving on, confirm what was decided.


======================
## How to Participate Effectively

  - Come prepared              : Read the agenda and pre-reads.
  - Speak up early             : One sentence in the first 5 min makes it easier.
  - Be concise                 : Make your point, give one reason, stop.
  - Listen actively            : Don't prepare your response while others are talking.
  - Disagree constructively    : "I see it differently — here's my thinking..."
  - Avoid dominating           : Give others space to speak.
  - Ask clarifying questions   : "Can you elaborate on that?" / "What does success look like?"


======================
## After the Meeting

  - Send meeting notes         : Within 24 hours.
  - Capture action items clearly:
      - What needs to be done
      - Who is responsible
      - By when (deadline)
  - Follow up on blockers      : Don't let action items go stale.
  - Cancel if no longer needed : Don't meet just to meet.


======================
## Meeting Notes Template

  Meeting        : [Title]
  Date           : [Date]
  Attendees      : [Names]

  Summary        : [2-3 line recap of what was discussed]

  Decisions Made :
    1. [Decision 1]
    2. [Decision 2]

  Action Items   :
    - [Task]     | Owner: [Name] | Due: [Date]
    - [Task]     | Owner: [Name] | Due: [Date]

  Parking Lot    : [Topics deferred to a future meeting]

  Next Meeting   : [Date & Time if applicable]


======================
## Common Meeting Anti-Patterns (What to Avoid)

  - No agenda                  : Meeting wanders, nothing gets decided.
  - Too many attendees         : More than 7 people slows decisions.
  - Running over time          : Disrespects everyone's schedule.
  - No action items            : Meeting ends with no clear next steps.
  - Recurring meetings with no purpose : Cancel or reduce frequency.
  - Multitasking               : Phones and laptops kill engagement.
  - HiPPO effect               : Highest Paid Person's Opinion dominates — encourage all voices.


======================
## Golden Rules for Meetings

  1. Every meeting needs a clear objective — if you can't state it, cancel the meeting.
  2. End with action items — who does what by when.
  3. Respect the clock — start and end on time.
  4. Fewer people = faster decisions.
  5. If it can be an email, make it an email.
  6. Follow up in writing after every important meeting.
  7. Prepare more than you think you need to.
