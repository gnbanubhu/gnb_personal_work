CHAT COMMUNICATION GUIDE
=========================

## What is Chat Communication?

  Chat communication covers all real-time and async text-based
  messaging in professional settings — Slack, Microsoft Teams,
  Google Chat, WhatsApp (work groups), and similar platforms.

  It is fast, informal, and easy to misread.
  Used well, it speeds up work. Used poorly, it creates noise and confusion.


=========================
## GENERAL PRINCIPLES

  - Be clear and concise       : Get to the point quickly.
  - One topic per message      : Don't mix multiple asks in one message.
  - Use threads                : Keep conversations organized and easy to follow.
  - Respect availability       : Not every message needs an instant reply.
  - Choose the right channel   : DM for private, channel for team-wide topics.
  - Don't type in anger        : Step away, cool down, then respond.
  - Read before you send       : Re-read to check tone and clarity.


=========================
## CHAT ETIQUETTE

  Do's:
    - Use the person's name when addressing them directly.
    - Acknowledge messages with a quick reply or emoji reaction.
    - Set your status (available / busy / in a meeting / OOO).
    - Use @mention only when that person specifically needs to see it.
    - Keep it professional even in casual chats.
    - Use bullet points for multiple items — walls of text are hard to read.

  Don'ts:
    - Don't just say "Hi" and wait — state your question immediately.
      Wrong : "Hi"  [5 min gap]  "Are you free?"  [5 min gap]  "I had a question..."
      Right : "Hi [Name], quick question — can you review PR #42 by EOD?"
    - Don't use ALL CAPS — it reads as shouting.
    - Don't send multiple one-word messages in a row.
      Wrong : "Hey" → "Quick question" → "About the report" → "Do you have it?"
      Right : "Hey, do you have the report ready? Need it for the 3pm meeting."
    - Don't use chat for long or sensitive conversations — use a call or email.
    - Don't expect immediate replies outside working hours.
    - Don't forward private conversations without permission.


=========================
## MESSAGE FORMATS

### Asking for Help
  "Hi [Name], could you help me with [specific issue]?
  I've already tried [what you tried]. Let me know when you're free."

### Making a Request
  "Hi [Name], could you [action] by [time/date]?
  Context: [1 line reason]. Let me know if you need anything from me."

### Sharing an Update
  "Quick update on [topic]:
  - Done    : [what's complete]
  - In Progress : [what's ongoing]
  - Blocker : [any issue] / None
  Tagging [Name] in case it affects your work."

### Asking a Question
  "Hi [Name], [direct question]?
  Background: [1–2 lines of context if needed]."

### Flagging an Urgent Issue
  "@[Name] — Heads up: [issue] is affecting [impact].
  I'm [what you're doing about it]. Will update in [timeframe]."

### Giving a Heads-Up
  "FYI — [brief info]. No action needed from your end, just keeping you in the loop."

### Following Up
  "Hi [Name], following up on [topic] from [day/date].
  Could you let me know the status? Happy to help if there's a blocker."

### Declining or Pushing Back
  "Hi [Name], I won't be able to [request] by [time] — I'm currently handling [other priority].
  I can get to it by [alternate time]. Does that work?"


=========================
## TONE IN CHAT

  Chat lacks facial expressions and voice tone — words carry everything.

  Comes across as rude:
    "Why didn't you do this?"
    "That's wrong."
    "No."

  Better versions:
    "I noticed this wasn't done — is there a blocker I can help with?"
    "I think there may be an issue here — can we take a look together?"
    "That won't work for this case. Here's what I suggest instead: [suggestion]."

  Sarcasm and jokes:
    - Avoid sarcasm in text — it almost always misreads.
    - Light humor is fine with close colleagues, but read the room.
    - When in doubt, leave it out.


=========================
## EMOJI & REACTIONS

  Used well, emojis add warmth and speed up communication.
  Overused, they look unprofessional.

  Common professional uses:
    👍  — acknowledged / agree / will do
    ✅  — done / completed
    🙏  — thanks
    👀  — I'm looking into it / I've seen this
    🔁  — please share / please forward
    ⏳  — working on it
    🚨  — urgent / flag

  Avoid in formal or senior-leader chats:
    😂 🤣 😜 🥳 in serious or escalation threads.


=========================
## PLATFORM-SPECIFIC TIPS

### Slack
  - Use channels for team topics, DMs for personal ones.
  - Reply in threads to avoid flooding the main channel.
  - Pin important messages in channels for easy reference.
  - Use /remind to set reminders for yourself.
  - Set Do Not Disturb (DND) during focus time.

### Microsoft Teams
  - Use @mentions to alert specific people in a channel.
  - Use "Reply" in a conversation thread, not a new message.
  - Use Urgent delivery for truly time-sensitive messages.
  - Keep channels organized by project or topic.
  - Use status messages to communicate availability.

### WhatsApp (Work Groups)
  - Keep it professional — assume all messages are visible to everyone.
  - Don't add people to groups without asking first.
  - Mute non-urgent groups during work hours.
  - Avoid late-night messages unless urgent.
  - Use "Reply" to a specific message for clarity in busy groups.


=========================
## ASYNC vs REAL-TIME CHAT

  Use Async (don't expect immediate reply) for:
    - Updates and FYIs
    - Non-urgent questions
    - Sharing documents or links
    - Status reports

  Use Real-Time (expect quick reply) for:
    - Urgent blockers
    - Quick back-and-forth decisions
    - Live collaboration

  When in doubt — ask:
    "Is this a good time to chat, or should I send you a message?"


=========================
## WHEN TO SWITCH FROM CHAT TO ANOTHER CHANNEL

  Switch to a Call when:
    - The chat has gone back and forth more than 5 times.
    - The topic is emotional or sensitive.
    - You need to show something (screen share).
    - There's a misunderstanding that keeps growing.

  Switch to Email when:
    - The message needs a formal record.
    - You're communicating with external parties.
    - The content is long, detailed, or needs attachments.

  Switch to a Meeting when:
    - Multiple stakeholders need to align.
    - A decision needs group input.
    - The issue is complex and needs discussion.


=========================
## GOLDEN RULES OF CHAT COMMUNICATION

  1. Don't make people wait for your question — ask it upfront.
  2. Short messages, clear intent — don't make people decode you.
  3. Threads keep conversations clean — always use them.
  4. Respond within a reasonable time — silence reads as ignoring.
  5. Tone is everything in text — when in doubt, be warmer.
  6. Not everything needs a chat — call when it's faster or sensitive.
  7. Respect boundaries — don't expect replies outside work hours.
  8. Less noise = more signal — don't over-message or over-notify.
