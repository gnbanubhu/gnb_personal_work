INTERPERSONAL COMMUNICATION GUIDE
==================================

## Core Communication Formats

### 1. The SBI Model (Situation → Behavior → Impact)
Best for: feedback, difficult conversations

  "In [situation], when you [behavior], it [impact]."

  Example:
  "In yesterday's meeting, when you interrupted me,
  it made it hard for me to finish my point."


### 2. NVC — Nonviolent Communication
Best for: conflict resolution, emotional conversations

  Observation → Feeling → Need → Request

  Example:
  "When the report was late [obs], I felt stressed [feeling]
  because I need reliability [need].
  Could we agree on a deadline process? [request]"


### 3. The PREP Format
Best for: presenting ideas, persuasion

  Point → Reason → Example → Point (restate)

  Example:
  "We should adopt daily standups. [Point]
  They reduce blockers early. [Reason]
  Our last sprint saw 30% fewer delays with them. [Example]
  That's why I recommend daily standups." [Point]


### 4. STAR Format
Best for: sharing experiences, interviews, updates

  Situation → Task → Action → Result


==================================
## Quick Principles

  - Listen actively   : Reflect back before responding
  - Be direct         : State your need clearly, don't hint
  - Separate facts from feelings : "I feel X" vs "You did Y"
  - Assume positive intent : Start from good faith
  - Close the loop    : Confirm understanding at the end


==================================
## Written Communication (Email / Slack / Docs)

  1. Subject/opener  — state the purpose in one line
  2. Context         — 1-2 sentences of background
  3. Ask or update   — be specific about what you need
  4. Deadline        — if applicable
  5. Next step       — who does what by when


==================================
## Most Practical Starting Points

  - SBI   → for giving or receiving feedback
  - PREP  → for sharing ideas or making a case
  These two cover 80% of everyday professional situations.
